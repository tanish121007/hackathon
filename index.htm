Historic Monuments

Taj Mahal

An iconic symbol of love, this mausoleum in Agra is renowned for its stunning white marble architecture.

Red Fort

Located in Delhi, the Red Fort is a historic fortification and a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Qutub Minar

A towering minaret in Delhi, it is an excellent example of Indo-Islamic Afghan architecture.

Festivals of India

Diwali

Known as the Festival of Lights, Diwali celebrates the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi

The Festival of Colors, Holi is celebrated with vibrant colors, music, and dance, marking the arrival of spring.

Navratri

A festival dedicated to the worship of the Hindu deity Durga, Navratri involves fasting, dance, and prayers over nine nights.

Traditional Cuisine

Butter Chicken

A rich and creamy dish, Butter Chicken is a popular North Indian curry made with marinated chicken cooked in a spiced tomato gravy.

Samosa

A savory pastry filled with spiced potatoes and peas, Samosas are a beloved snack across India.

Biryani

A fragrant rice dish layered with marinated meat and spices, Biryani is a staple of Indian cuisine with regional variations.
